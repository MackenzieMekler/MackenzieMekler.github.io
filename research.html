My Research Experiences

Brusko Lab

I entered Brusko lab in Fall of 2021, my first year. Here I learned basic wet lab techniques, handeled cell cultures, was resposnible for biohazerdous waste, and more.

After some time, I began working with a masters degree student and helped with his project. The project tested the effect of TCR knockouts on CAR regulatory T Cells.

At the end of this experience, I helped turn this data into a poster where we concluded that CRISPR/Cas9 Knockouts were a viable method to reduce TCR expression on the transduced cells without harming the population viability.

Swanson Lab

I began working in the Swanson lab in the Fall of 2022. My tasks involved analyzing videos of mice, running PCR/Gels, and conducting immunofluorescence experiments.

I was first involved in a project studying strokes in the African Spiny Mouse. This requried brain staining, immunofluorescence, and analysis of videos. During this time I began my honors thesis on how myotonic dystrophy affects neural stem cell populations.

Bonning Lab

I entered Bonning lab working for Dr. Wu as a lab assistant duing the Fall of 2022. Here I assisted in a project focused on establishing an immortal cell line for the study of Citrus Greening Disease.

My main responsibilities in this lab revolved around cell culture. I was responsible for changing media, analyzing cells, passaging cells, and anything else required to maintain the cultures. Additional responsibilities included dissections to establish new cultures as well as RNA extraction and PCRs.
